About ADAS

ADAS stands for Advanced Driver Assistance Systems. These systems use cameras, radar sensors and steering sensors to improve vehicle safety.

Common ADAS features include:

  • Lane Keep Assist
  • Adaptive Cruise Control
  • Automatic Emergency Braking
  • Blind Spot Monitoring
  • Traffic Sign Recognition

When does ADAS need calibration?

Calibration may be required after:

  • Windscreen replacement
  • Wheel alignment or suspension repairs
  • Accident or bumper repairs
  • Steering adjustments
  • Steering Angle Sensor (SAS) reset or replacement, or if the vehicle is pulling to one side or the steering wheel is not centered after repairs
