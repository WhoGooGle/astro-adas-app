Fast ADAS Calibration Trigger Checklist

If ANY item is “Yes”, calibration is typically required/recommended (OEM dependent).

  • Windscreen replaced (forward camera area)
  • Bumper/accident repair near radar or front camera
  • Radar/camera removed, replaced or disturbed
  • Wheel alignment performed on ADAS vehicle
  • Suspension repairs / ride height change
  • SAS reset/replacement or steering work done
  • Tyre size change (diameter/profile change)

Tip: no warning light ≠ correct calibration.

Steering Wheel Off-Centre / Pulling After Repairs (SAS focus)

Common after: alignment, suspension, steering rack work, subframe shift, accident repair.
Step-by-step workflow
  • 1) Road test on flat road: confirm pull, drift, steering wheel angle at straight.
  • 2) Tyres first: pressures equal, tyre condition, swap L/R front if needed to isolate tyre pull.
  • 3) Alignment check: toe, camber, caster, rear thrust angle. Verify steering wheel centred mechanically.
  • 4) Steering angle sensor (SAS) values: check live data at straight ahead (should be ~0°).
  • 5) SAS reset / relearn only after steering wheel is mechanically centred + alignment within spec.
  • 6) ADAS systems: lane assist / stability systems rely on SAS. Recalibrate/verify as required.
If wheel is mechanically centred but SAS shows offset: perform SAS calibration/relearn.

Quick Diagnostic Logic (Use in the bay)

Vehicle pulls to one side
  • Check tyre pressure + tyre conicity (swap front tyres L/R)
  • Check brake drag (front calipers, rear brakes)
  • Check alignment (toe/camber/caster) + rear thrust angle
  • Check ride height differences (spring sag, uneven load)
  • If ADAS lane keep is fighting steering: verify SAS + camera calibration
Steering wheel off-centre after alignment
  • Alignment may be “in spec” but steering wheel not centred: correct toe split properly
  • Ensure rack centred before toe adjustment
  • Verify SAS straight-ahead value (should be ~0°)
  • Perform SAS relearn if required by vehicle
ADAS warnings after repairs
  • Scan for codes and note which ADAS module is affected
  • Check sensor mounting position/aim (camera/radar/brackets)
  • Confirm windscreens are correct spec (camera bracket/area)
  • Perform calibration procedure (static/dynamic/both)
  • Clear codes and confirm operation with a road verification
