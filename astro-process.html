The Astro Tyres ADAS Calibration Process

At Astro Tyres, ADAS calibration is performed using a structured process to ensure safety systems work according to manufacturer specifications.

ADAS systems rely on millimetre-level accuracy. Even small changes after repairs can affect how the vehicle brakes, steers, and warns the driver.

Step 1 — Vehicle condition check

  • Tyre condition & correct pressures
  • Suspension and steering condition
  • Ride height / vehicle stance
  • Basic alignment condition (where required)

Step 2 — Diagnostic scan

  • Scan for ADAS and safety system fault codes
  • Identify the required calibration procedures
  • Confirm system readiness

Step 3 — Precision workshop setup

  • Correct vehicle positioning
  • Accurate target placement (static calibration where required)
  • Correct distances and centreline alignment

This step is critical — calibration accuracy depends on correct setup.

Step 4 — Calibration procedure

  • Static calibration (with targets in the workshop)
  • Dynamic calibration (driving procedure where required)
  • Some vehicles require both

Step 5 — Steering Angle Sensor (SAS) verification

  • Confirm steering wheel is centred
  • Verify SAS values are correct
  • Reset/relearn SAS if required

Incorrect SAS can cause lane assist and stability systems to behave incorrectly.

Step 6 — Final verification

  • Confirm calibration success on diagnostic system
  • Clear codes if applicable and recheck data
  • Confirm systems operate correctly

Step 7 — Customer feedback & proof

  • Explain what was calibrated and why it was needed
  • Provide confirmation of completed calibration (where available)

Why professional calibration matters

  • Correct hazard detection
  • Correct braking and distance judgement
  • Correct lane positioning and warnings
  • Confidence that systems work as designed
