Vehicles with ADAS in South Africa (Including Chinese brands)

Most vehicles from 2018 onwards may include at least one ADAS feature (depending on model/trim).

If your vehicle has lane assist, adaptive cruise, emergency braking, blind spot monitoring, or camera-based safety features, it likely has ADAS.

Common ADAS features you may see

Lane Assist AEB (Auto Braking) Adaptive Cruise Blind Spot Forward Collision Warning Traffic Sign Recognition Rear Cross Traffic Alert

Popular brands in SA that often have ADAS

Examples below are common in SA. Exact features depend on model and trim level.

Japanese & Korean

  • Toyota (Corolla Cross, RAV4, Fortuner newer trims)
  • Nissan (X-Trail, Qashqai, some Navara trims)
  • Mazda (CX-5, CX-30)
  • Honda (CR-V, Civic newer trims)
  • Hyundai (Tucson, Santa Fe, i30 N-line trims)
  • Kia (Sportage, Sorento, Seltos trims)

European

  • Volkswagen (Tiguan, Golf 8, T-Roc trims)
  • Mercedes-Benz (C-Class, GLC, A-Class newer)
  • BMW (3 Series, X1/X3 trims)
  • Audi (A3, Q3/Q5 trims)
  • Volvo (XC40/60/90 – strong ADAS presence)

South Africa’s fast-growing Chinese brands

  • Haval (Jolion, H6)
  • GWM (P-Series, Tank models where fitted)
  • Chery (Tiggo 7 Pro, Tiggo 8 Pro)
  • Omoda (C5)
  • Jaecoo (J7)
  • BAIC (Beijing X55)
  • Foton / other emerging brands (model dependent)

Chinese SUVs often include ADAS as standard on many trims, which is why calibration awareness is important in SA.

Fast self-check

  • Look behind the rear-view mirror for a forward camera
  • Look at the front badge/grille area for a radar panel
  • Check the infotainment menu for “Driver Assistance / Safety”

If you’ve had a windscreen replacement, wheel alignment, suspension or bumper repair, your ADAS may require calibration.
