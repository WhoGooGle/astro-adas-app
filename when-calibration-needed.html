When does ADAS need calibration?

ADAS calibration may be required after common repairs that affect the vehicle's geometry or sensor position.

Calibration may be required after:

  • Windscreen replacement
  • Wheel alignment
  • Suspension repairs
  • Steering repairs
  • Accident damage or bumper repairs
  • Ride height changes
  • Tyre size changes
  • Camera or radar replacement
  • Steering Angle Sensor (SAS) reset or replacement
  • If the vehicle is pulling to one side or the steering wheel is not centred

Why calibration is important

If sensors are not correctly aligned, safety systems may:

  • Brake too early or too late
  • Misjudge distance to other vehicles
  • Fail to detect lane markings correctly
  • Provide incorrect warnings
